# 1

Sabbihisma Rabbikal A'laa ^qTrans860


EXTOL the limitless glory of thy Sustainer's name: [the glory of] the Al-Highest, ^qAsad860


Praise the name of thy Lord the Most High, ^qPickthall860


Magnify the Name of thy Lord the Most High ^qArberry860

# 2

Allazee khalaqa fasawwaa ^qTrans861


who creates [every thing], and thereupon forms it in accordance with what it is meant to be, ^qAsad861


Who createth, then disposeth; ^qPickthall861


who created and shaped, ^qArberry861

# 3

Wallazee qaddara fahadaa ^qTrans862


and who determines the nature [of all that exists], and thereupon guides it [towards its fulfilment], ^qAsad862


Who measureth, then guideth; ^qPickthall862


who determined and guided, ^qArberry862

# 4

Wallazeee akhrajal mar'aa ^qTrans863


and who brings forth herbage, ^qAsad863


Who bringeth forth the pasturage, ^qPickthall863


who brought forth the pasturage ^qArberry863

# 5

Faja'alahoo ghusaaa'an ahwaa ^qTrans864


and thereupon causes it to decay into rust-brown stubble! ^qAsad864


Then turneth it to russet stubble. ^qPickthall864


then made it a blackening wrack. ^qArberry864

# 6

Sanuqri'uka falaa tansaaa ^qTrans865


WE SHALL teach thee, and thou wilt not forget [aught of what thou art taught], ^qAsad865


We shall make thee read (O Muhammad) so that thou shalt not forget ^qPickthall865


We shall make thee recite, to forget not ^qArberry865

# 7

Illaa maa shaaa'al laah; innahoo ya'lamul jahra wa maa yakhfaa ^qTrans866


save what God may will [thee to forget] - for, verily, He [alone] knows all that is open to [man's] perception as well as all that is hidden [from it] ^qAsad866


Save that which Allah willeth. Lo! He knoweth the disclosed and that which still is hidden; ^qPickthall866


save what God wills; surely He knows what is spoken aloud and what is hidden. ^qArberry866

# 8

Wa nu-yassiruka lilyusraa ^qTrans867


and [thus] shall We make easy for thee the path towards [ultimate] ease. ^qAsad867


And We shall ease thy way unto the state of ease. ^qPickthall867


We shall ease thee unto the Easing. ^qArberry867

# 9

Fazakkir in nafa'atizzikraa ^qTrans868


REMIND, THEN, [others of the truth, regardless of] whether this reminding [would seem to] be of use [or not]: ^qAsad868


Therefor remind (men), for of use is the reminder. ^qPickthall868


Therefore remind, if the Reminder profits, ^qArberry868

# 10

Sa yazzakkaru maiyakhshaa ^qTrans869


in mind will keep it he who stands in awe [of God], ^qAsad869


He will heed who feareth, ^qPickthall869


and he who fears shall remember, ^qArberry869

# 11

Wa yatajannabuhal ashqaa ^qTrans8610


but aloof from it will remain that most hapless wretch – ^qAsad8610


But the most hapless will flout it, ^qPickthall8610


but the most wretched shall flout it, ^qArberry8610

# 12

Allazee yaslan Naaral kubraa ^qTrans8611


he who [in the life to come] shall have to endure the great fire ^qAsad8611


He who will be flung to the great Fire ^qPickthall8611


even he who shall roast in the Great Fire, ^qArberry8611

# 13

Summa laa yamootu feehaa wa laa yahyaa ^qTrans8612


wherein he will neither die nor remain alive. ^qAsad8612


Wherein he will neither die nor live. ^qPickthall8612


then he shall neither die therein, nor live. ^qArberry8612

# 14

Qad aflaha man tazakkaa ^qTrans8613


To happiness [in the life to come] will indeed attain he who attains to purity [in this world], ^qAsad8613


He is successful who groweth, ^qPickthall8613


Prosperous is he who has cleansed himself, ^qArberry8613

# 15

Wa zakaras ma Rabbihee fasallaa ^qTrans8614


and remembers his Sustainer's name, and prays [unto Him]. ^qAsad8614


And remembereth the name of his Lord, so prayeth, ^qPickthall8614


and mentions the Name of his Lord, and prays. ^qArberry8614

# 16

Bal tu'siroonal hayaatad dunyaa ^qTrans8615


But nay, [O men,] you prefer the life of this world, ^qAsad8615


But ye prefer the life of the world ^qPickthall8615


Nay, but you prefer the present life; ^qArberry8615

# 17

Wal Aakhiratu khairunw wa abqaa ^qTrans8616


although the life to come is better and more enduring. ^qAsad8616


Although the Hereafter is better and more lasting. ^qPickthall8616


and the world to come is better, and more enduring. ^qArberry8616

# 18

Inna haazaa lafis suhu fil oolaa ^qTrans8617


Verily, [all] this has indeed been [said] in the earlier revelations – ^qAsad8617


Lo! This is in the former scrolls. ^qPickthall8617


Surely this is in the ancient scrolls, ^qArberry8617

# 19

Suhufi Ibraaheema wa Moosaa ^qTrans8618


the revelations of Abraham and Moses. ^qAsad8618


The Books of Abraham and Moses. ^qPickthall8618


the scrolls of Abraham and Moses. ^qArberry8618
